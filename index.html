AB Rubber Mills —

01

The Situation

AB Rubber Mills had been making rubber rolls for rice de-husking for 40 years. For five of those years, revenue grew at 2–3% annually and nobody could explain why it wasn't growing faster.

Every decision — pricing, ordering, dispatch, customer management — sat in the CEO's head. One accounting software. Paper files going back decades. No data anyone could actually use. The business worked. But it had no visibility into itself and no capacity to grow.

02

The Diagnosis

First step: talk to everyone, map every workflow end to end. The data existed — scattered across decades of paper files. An admin staff member spent a month digitizing 20 years of sales, purchase, and GST records. The sales team documented their activity for 30 days.

Four root causes emerged:

  • Sales bandwidth entirely consumed by existing clients — no capacity for new acquisition
  • Admin time dominated by manual tasks that could be automated: invoicing, transport documentation, GST filing
  • Underpricing on several product lines, with long repayment schedules compressing margins further
  • Demand concentrated in Punjab — seasonal gaps with no buffer and no geographic spread
03

What Was Built

Three phases, built progressively. Each phase had to prove its value before the next began.

Phase 1 — Custom ERP/CRM

Order intake, sales mobile module, production dashboard, management view. Single source of truth for the first time. Built using no-code tools and deployed in weeks.

Phase 2 — Tally + GST Integration

Automated invoicing, transport challan, builty generation. Dispatch no longer dependent on one person being in the office. Admin manual workload reduced to 20% of prior volume.

Phase 3 — AI Layer

Demand forecasting using 20 years of historical data. AI production scheduling with delay alerts. Automated dispatch documentation. Weekly AI-generated management brief covering production, inventory, sales, financials, and external market signals.

Raw material procurement: AI agents tracking commodity prices and currency signals to time purchases. 10-year historical data used to identify seasonal price patterns. Result: 5–6% raw material cost savings.

04

The Commercial Shift

70–75% of new revenue came from new or dormant customers. The targeting approach: reach customers just before their peak demand cycle using historical data by region and rice varietal processed.

New customers were sampled during their off-season — when they had free capacity to run tests and low pressure to commit. Outreach intensified two months before their season, with orders confirmed one month before to give production enough lead time.

Pricing corrections and shortened repayment schedules contributed the remaining margin improvement alongside raw material savings and economies of scale.

05

The Handover

Everything built is documented and team-owned. The sales team receives weekly AI-generated customer targets. Production runs off the ERP. Management reviews a weekly intelligence brief instead of firefighting daily operations.

The business runs without daily management involvement. The CEO now has bandwidth to think about the next phase — including entering the Cambodian market.

That was the goal from day one.

Running a business that's been flat too long?

Flat revenue is usually a symptom. The underlying problems vary — margins, bandwidth, ops overhead, data blindness. The diagnostic finds the actual root cause.
