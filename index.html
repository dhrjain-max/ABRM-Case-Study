01

The Situation

A 40-year-old manufacturing business making rubber rolls for rice de-husking.

For five years, revenue grew at 2–3% annually. Nobody could explain why it wasn't growing faster.

Every decision — pricing, ordering, dispatch, customer management — sat in the CEO's head. One accounting package. Paper files going back decades.

The business worked. It just had no visibility into itself and no capacity to grow.

02

The Diagnosis

First step: talk to everyone. Map every workflow end to end.

The data existed — scattered across decades of paper files. An admin staff member spent a month digitizing 20 years of records. The sales team documented their activity for 30 days.

Four root causes emerged:

  • Sales bandwidth entirely consumed by existing clients — no capacity for new acquisition
  • Admin time dominated by manual tasks: invoicing, transport docs, GST filing — all automatable
  • Underpricing on several lines, with long repayment schedules compressing margins further
  • Demand concentrated in one region — seasonal gaps, no buffer, no geographic spread
03

What Was Built

Three phases, built progressively. Each proved its value before the next began.

Phase 1 — Custom ERP/CRM

Order intake, sales mobile module, production dashboard, management view. Single source of truth for the first time. Built in weeks.

Phase 2 — Tally + GST Integration

Automated invoicing, transport challan, builty generation. Admin workload down to 20% of prior volume. Dispatch no longer dependent on one person.

Phase 3 — AI Layer

Demand forecasting from 20 years of data. Production scheduling with delay alerts. Weekly AI management brief covering ops, sales, inventory, and market signals.

AI agents tracking commodity prices and currency signals to time raw material purchases. Result: 5–6% cost savings.

04

The Commercial Shift

70–75% of new revenue came from new or dormant customers.

70–75% of new revenue from customers who weren't previously buying

The approach: reach customers just before their peak demand cycle, using historical data by region and crop varietal.

Sample during off-season — when they have capacity to test, no pressure to commit. Intensify outreach two months before their season starts.

Pricing corrections and shorter repayment schedules drove the margin improvement. Geographic expansion smoothed the seasonal gaps.

05

The Handover

Everything built is documented and team-owned.

Sales team gets weekly AI-generated customer targets. Production runs off the ERP. Management reviews a weekly intelligence brief instead of firefighting daily operations.

The business runs without daily management involvement. The CEO now has bandwidth to think about expansion — including entering the Cambodian market.

That was the goal from day one.
